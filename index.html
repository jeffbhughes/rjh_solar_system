Sol(Sun)

Facts

The Sun, also known as Sol, is the largest star from our solar system. If we look at the sun it looks pretty big even though it is considered a yellow dwarf star. The name dwarf can be deceiving because the Sun is 1,160 times bigger than the biggest planet in our solar system.

Mercury

Facts

Mercury is one of only 2 planets in our solar system to have 0 moons. Mercury is the first of four terrestrial planets. The earth is 2.6 times bigger than mercury with a diameter of 1,516 miles. On mercury for 1 day to occur it takes 59 days on earth. For mercury to complete 1 rotation of the sun it takes 88 days on earth.

Venus

Facts

Venus is the second planet in our solar system. Venus is a terrestrial planet. The planet venus has no moons. Venus is one of two planets to rotate clockwise. It takes 243 earth days for only 1 day on venus. It takes longer for 1 day to occur on venus than 1 year on venus. One Venus year is equal to 225 earth days. Venus has a very similar shape and structure to earth giving it the name, earth's twin.

Earth

Facts

The earth is the third planet in our solar system. The earth is the only planet with confirmed life in the milky way galaxy. The earth has only one moon. The earth is the largest terrestrial planet. It is also the densest planet in our solar system. A day on earth is 24 hours long, and a year is 365.25 days.

Mars

Facts

Mars is about half the size of earth. Mars is also the least dense planet out of all 4 terrestrial planets. Mars has seasons just like earth because of the tilt of its axis. 1 day on mars is 24 hours and 37 minutes. 1 year on mars is 687 days. Mars has 2 major moons.

Jupiter

Facts

Jupiter is the biggest planet in our solar system. The planet Jupiter is the first of four gas giants. A day on Jupiter is only 10 hours, and 1 year on jupiter is 4,331 days. Jupiter has a faint set of rings. Jupiter has 95 moons and the biggest moon in our solar system. Jupiter has 91 minor moons and 4 major moons.

Saturn

Facts

The gaseous planet, Saturn, has the most moons of any planet in the solar system. Saturn also has rings made out of primarily chunks of ice and rocks. 1 day on Saturn is 10 hours and 33 minutes and 1 year on Saturn is 10,756 days. Saturn has 146 moons. 139 of Saturn's moons are minor and 7 are major.

Uranus

Facts

The gaseous planet, Uranus, rotates on a 90 degree angle making it appear to spin sideways. The size of Uranus is nearly 14 times bigger than the earth and has a gravitational pull of 0.886. Uranus has 27 moons including 5 major and 22 minor moons. 1 day on Uranus is equal to 17 hours and 14 minutes on earth. 1 year on Uranus is equal to 30,687 days.

Neptune

Facts

The gaseous planet, Neptune, is the last planet in our solar system. Neptune is the furthest planet from the sun at almost 2.8 billion miles away. Because of how far away it is this makes the years on Neptune a lot longer at 60,190 days on earth. The days on Neptune are a lot shorter though at only 16 hours and 6 minutes. Neptune has 14 moons including 1 major moon and 13 minor moons.
